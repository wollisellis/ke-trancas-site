**Background:** Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as transformative tools in healthcare, offering applications in clinical decision support, medical education, patient communication, and research.

**Objective:** This systematic review synthesizes current evidence on LLM applications in healthcare settings.

**Methods:** We searched OpenAlex and PubMed for peer-reviewed articles (2020-2026) using comprehensive search terms. Papers were screened for relevance to both LLM technology and healthcare applications.

## 4. Discussion

### 4.1 Principal Findings

This review identifies **1055 studies** examining LLM applications in healthcare, representing rapid growth in this field. Key findings:

1. **Clinical Decision Support:** LLMs show promise in diagnostic assistance and clinical prediction, though validation studies remain limited.

2. **Documentation:** Automated clinical note generation and summarization could reduce administrative burden.

3. **Medical Education:** Interactive learning tools and question-answering systems enhance trainee education.

4. **Patient Communication:** Chatbots and patient-facing applications require careful safety considerations.

### 4.2 Challenges Identified

**Accuracy & Hallucination:**
- Models may generate confident but incorrect information
- Clinical validation essential before deployment

**Bias & Fairness:**
- Training data biases may propagate into recommendations
- Underrepresented populations may receive suboptimal care

**Privacy & Security:**
- Patient data protection requirements
- Secure deployment architectures needed

**Regulatory Uncertainty:**
- FDA/CE marking pathways unclear for LLM-based tools
- Liability frameworks undefined

### 4.3 Limitations

**This review:**
- Rapidly evolving field limits comprehensiveness
- Many studies are descriptive rather than evaluative
- Lack of standardized outcome measures

**Literature:**
- Publication bias toward positive results
- Limited long-term follow-up studies
- Geographic concentration in high-income countries

---

## 5. Conclusions

Large Language Models represent a significant opportunity for healthcare transformation across multiple domains. However, responsible deployment requires:

1. **Rigorous validation** in clinical settings
2. **Bias mitigation** strategies
3. **Regulatory frameworks** specific to LLM applications
4. **Transparent reporting** of model limitations

Future research should prioritize:
- Prospective clinical trials
- Standardized evaluation metrics
- Real-world implementation studies
- Ethical and regulatory frameworks
